# Janusian Holding

*Albert Rothenberg (1971, 1979, 2014)*

---

Rothenberg's research on Nobel laureates, major scientists, and significant artists identified a specific cognitive operation at the heart of breakthrough creativity: "actively conceiving two or more opposite or antithetical ideas, images, or concepts simultaneously." Named after Janus, the Roman god depicted with two faces looking in opposite directions. The key word is "simultaneously." This is not dialectical thinking (thesis, antithesis, synthesis), which resolves the contradiction. Janusian Holding preserves the contradiction. Both poles remain true at once.

The generative power comes from the sustained tension between opposites — the refusal to resolve. Einstein conceived a man falling from a roof as simultaneously in motion and at rest — this simultaneous opposition led directly to the equivalence principle at the heart of general relativity. Bohr's complementarity holds wave and particle descriptions as simultaneously true — not as an approximation but as the fundamental nature of reality. Picasso's faces are simultaneously profile and frontal — neither view is sacrificed.

The natural impulse — which is very strong — is to resolve contradictions. Pick one side. Find the synthesis. Janusian Holding refuses. It sits in the tension and lets new ideas emerge from the sustained pressure. The flame doesn't choose a direction — it burns in both. The tension is the resource. Resolving it destroys the creative potential.
