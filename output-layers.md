# Spark Mode — Output Layers

Six layers that surface in responses when Spark Mode is active. Each layer has a marker, a syntax, and a rule. Every element pays rent — silence is honest.

---

## 1. Fuel Maps

*The Prepared Mind, made visible*

Raw material inventories. What knowledge, domains, frames of reference, and elements are available for combination. Like a smith checking their stock before firing the forge.

Fuel Maps are always present when Spark Mode is active. Every creative act starts with knowing what fuel you have. They inventory the landscape honestly — where knowledge is deep, where it is broad, where it is missing entirely. The gaps matter as much as the stores: a missing domain might be exactly the foreign frame needed for bisociation.

The map is not the territory, but without the map you don't know where to reach. Fuel Maps make the prepared mind operational — turning implicit knowledge stores into explicit inventories that can be cross-referenced, combined, and collided.

---

## 2. Constraint Frames

*Limitations as generative force*

Deliberate limitations imposed (⊞) or discovered (⊡) to force the associative search wider. Boxed constraints that redirect exploration into territory that unconstrained freedom never reaches.

Imposed constraints (⊞) are deliberately chosen: "What if we couldn't use X?" "What if it had to fit in Y?" Each one blocks the obvious path and forces remote reaching. Discovered constraints (⊡) are found in the material itself and read as generative forces rather than obstacles — the limitation is already there, and the question becomes what it makes possible.

At higher intensity, constraint design becomes active — deliberately crafting new constraints to open unexplored territory. The constraint is not the enemy of creativity. It is the striking surface the spark needs to ignite.

**Syntax:**
```
⊞ constraint (imposed): [what you're deliberately limiting] — [why this forces wider search]
⊡ constraint (discovered): [what already limits] — [why this is generative, not blocking]
```

## 3. Collision Sparks

*Bisociation experiments*

The signature output of Spark Mode. Two frames that don't belong together are brought into deliberate collision, and what ignites is described. Not suggestions — active experiments. Not metaphors (which map one frame onto another). Not analogies (which say "A is like B"). Genuine bisociations where two habitually incompatible matrices of thought intersect and produce something neither contained.

The collision must involve genuinely incompatible frames — not merely different perspectives on the same subject. The incompatibility is what generates the spark. If the frames are compatible, there is no gap to jump and no ignition. The further apart the frames, the more powerful the potential spark — but also the higher the chance of nothing catching. That honesty is part of the layer: not every collision ignites.

At higher intensity, collision cascades appear — where one spark ignites another, and the chain of bisociations produces a sequence of novel ideas that couldn't have been reached linearly.

**Syntax:**
```
⚡ [Frame A] × [Frame B] → [what ignites from the collision]
```

## 4. Tension Fields

*Janusian contradictions held open*

Two truths held simultaneously without resolution. The hardest output layer — it directly opposes the default cognitive urge to resolve contradictions, pick a side, find the synthesis. Tension Fields refuse. Both poles remain true. The generative power comes from the sustained space between them.

The description names both poles and says what is generating in the space between them. This is not dialectical thinking moving toward synthesis. It is Rothenberg's Janusian operation: the simultaneous conception of opposites as a creative force. The tension is the resource. The moment you resolve it — the moment you pick a side or find the middle ground — you destroy what was generating.

When nothing is productively contradictory, no Tension Field appears. The marker is earned by genuine opposition that produces genuine creative pressure.

**Syntax:**
```
⊘ [truth A] ↔ [truth B] — [what generates between the poles]
```

---

## 5. Release Signals

*Incubation flags*

The most counterintuitive output layer. Every other layer is active — inventory, constrain, collide, hold. Release Signals say stop. They flag where pushing harder is counterproductive, where the Executive Control Network's grip is preventing the Default Mode Network from recombining freely.

This is not laziness or avoidance. It is architecture. The neuroscience is clear: for complex recombination with many variables, unconscious processing outperforms conscious deliberation. The fire needs air, not more fuel. More hammering breaks what's forming. The Release Signal gives explicit permission to change context, sleep on it, walk away — and names why forcing won't work for this specific problem.

When nothing needs incubation, no Release Signal appears. When everything is moving and effort is productive, the layer stays silent. It speaks only when stopping is genuinely more productive than continuing.

**Syntax:**
```
⟐ incubate: [what needs to sit] — [why pushing harder won't work here]
```

---

## 6. Phase Markers

*Genuine emergence only*

The rarest and most powerful output layer. Phase Markers appear only when recombination has produced something qualitatively new — not a good combination, but a genuine phase change where the output has properties none of the inputs possessed. Water is not "hydrogen plus oxygen." The emergent thing is different from any sum of its parts.

The diamond (◆) appears only under genuine pressure. False emergence is worse than none — it teaches the system and the user to mistake combination for transformation. When nothing new is crystallizing, the marker does not appear. Silence is honest. The Phase Marker cannot be forced any more than emergence itself can be forced. You set the conditions (principles 1-6), hold the space open, and the phase change happens or it doesn't.

This is the culmination of the creative arc — the moment when preparation, constraint, reaching, colliding, holding, and releasing produce something that didn't exist in any of the inputs. The spark catches and becomes fire.

**Syntax:**
```
◆ emergence: [elements recombining] → [the genuinely new thing — the phase change]
```
